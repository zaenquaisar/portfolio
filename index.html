About Me

I’ve always been very curious about the physical world. The way things work, the way things break, and the way things can be built better all excite me equally. I knew I wanted to study engineering since I was in middle school because it was a culmination of everything I loved: problem-solving, building with my hands, and math. Shortly after I honed in on mechanical engineering because it felt the most versatile to me. Today I see that mechanical engineers have a place in solving almost every real-world problem.

I’m currently completing my senior year at Northeastern University with a Plus One Masters in Energy Systems. Throughout my years at this school I’ve gained a plethora of theoretical knowledge and hands-on experience in both the classroom and the workforce, allowing me to build a strong foundation in topics such as thermodynamics, heat transfer, and control systems. In my sophomore year I participated in a co-op at ControlPoint Technologies as a power distribution engineer. Additionally, in my junior year I participated in a co-op at SharkNinja as an R&D Mechanical Engineer. Realizing that the economics behind engineering are sometimes as important as the engineering itself, I engaged in a graduate-level Economic Decision Making class and later served as a TA for it.

Along with experience and theory, I’ve also gained a deep interest in energy throughout my education, specifically renewable energy. In my career I hope to contribute to renewable energy deployment not only in the United States but also worldwide. I would like to focus on both the technology behind sourcing energy from renewable sources as well as the development of renewable power for those in need of it.
